Chicken Wontons with Spicy Sauce

Description

This is my new chicken wonton method – steaming them up with with a spicy sauce that is somewhere between dipping sauce and soup broth. It’s meant to be eaten in a bowl, like a soup – kinda, but it’s so much more than just broth with wontons. It’s luscious, dippable, scoopable, slurpable, and full of umami from the shiitake mushrooms and greens (like spinach or bok choy), if you choose to add some. The sesame oil gives even more depth, and the chili oil gives a bit of heat and a pop of color.

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons sesame oil, divided
  • 14 ounces shiitake mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 clove garlic, grated
  • one 12-ounce bag frozen wontons (I like the mini chicken
  • cilantro ones from Trader Joe’s or Costco)
  • 1–2 cups chicken broth (depending on how soupy you like it)
  • 1/2 cup teriyaki sauce
  • 1–2 tablespoons chili crisp
  • green onions, sliced
  • sesame seeds

Method

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon sesame oil in a large nonstick skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add mushrooms; sauté until softened and yummy.
  3. Add garlic for the last minute or two of sautéing.
  4. Add the frozen wontons, chicken broth, and teriyaki sauce.
  5. Simmer for 5 minutes, with a lid on, until the wontons are heated through.
  6. Finish with remaining 2 tablespoons sesame oil, chili crisp, and top with sesame seeds and scallions.
